HITTING, SETTING,
AND RECEIVING

There are many positions in volleyball, but they all center around one or more of these three fundamental skills: hitting, setting, and receiving. The ability to perform these skills well is paramount to a player's success. But, if you don't know how to do them, where do you start?

Well, you're in luck! Below you will find introductory videos to all three skills. After watching these (and practicing quite a bit), you should be pretty solid, from a mechanical perspective.

If you have any questions along the way when learning these skills, reach out to one of the officers on GroupMe. We're all here to help you get better, and would love the opportunity to do some coaching on the side.

Before you decide what skill to fully pursue (and what position to play), consider my personal take on personality types and positions. If you are physically gifted, power-focused, with the desire to slam a ball down, become a hitter. If you are analytically gifted, have dexterous hands and good acceleration capabilities, and want to control the court, become a setter. If you are shorter, with amazing acceleration capabilities, a willingness to put it all on the line to get the ball up, and good predictive abilities, become a libero. I personally hit for my high school team, set for my high school club, and have both set and hit for OKState (I will be playing outside this year).
