PLAYERS TO STUDY

There is no singular blueprint for success in volleyball. Different people have different bodies, and what may injure one player may be the best method for another. Below you will find a list of players with different physiques and playstyles to study and emulate, based off of your body type. This list is by no means comprehensive, and you are encouraged to find players that suit your style best online. However, this should point you in the right direction.

Hover a player to study them

Ran Takahashi

The blueprint for a short outside. At 6'2", he is the leader of the Japanese national team. His torso rotation is world-class, as is his technique and jumping ability. If you are a rather normal guy with dreams of hitting, this is the man you should study.

Matt Anderson

The epitome of the American style of Volleyball. Tall, well-built, athletic, with a near perfect swing form. If you are on the taller, slower side, but still wish to be an outside, study his game.

Kento Miyaura

A short, smart hitter. Has great musculature, but uses his brain to aim his shots and serves (I would have put Yuji Nishida, but I believe Miyaura's mind is greater than Nishida's muscle). If you are an expressly shorter, muscular player, study Miyaura.

Simon Torwie

The taller a middle becomes, the worse they are at anything but blocking and hitting (mostly). However, Torwie is a great example of a middle who can also set and serve exceptionally well for his frame. If you are large vertically and horizontally, and want to be a MB, study his serve, and how he slams his feet into the ground to send his heavy frame flying.

Cameron Thorne

The shortest middle blocker the USA National Team has ever seen (6'4"!). Still tall from an objective perspective, but relatively tiny on a professional level. If you are a shorter (below 6'4") aspiring middle, study the way he uses his body to swing cross shots, as well as how he blocks to make up for his height.

Cole Hartke

One of the largest right sides in the world (6'11"). None of you viewing this page are probably as big as him, but if you are extremely tall, and a little uncoordinated as a result, study the way Hartke swings and approaches. When you get to be ~6'6" and above, you start needing different training to take advantage of your frame, and Hartke is a great blueprint.

Micah Christenson

Arguably the greatest setter of all time, currently still playing for the USA. The absolute blueprint for technical mastery. If you want to be a setter that makes any team you're on greater, study his decision making and how he arcs the ball differently for each player's individual preferences.

Luciano De Cecco

Argentinian national setter; you will see at a glance that he looks a little big (and lowkey fat) to be a professional setter. But watching him play will quickly reveal to you that, although he is a bit slow, he more than makes up for it with great positioning and technique. If you are a budding setter who is a little bigger-boned, study how De Cecco seems to predict where his receiver will put the ball, so that he can get to it in time.

Tomohiro Yamamoto

My pick for the best libero of all time. Yamamoto's willingness to kill himself to get the ball up is extraordinary, and his predictive ability is top-notch. If you want to be a libero, study where he positions himself for the receive each time, and how he dives to absorb impact with the floor while still keeping the ball up.
